The RingBot

Overview

RingBot is a motion-control platform exploring ring-shaped locomotion and stability. The system integrates embedded control, motor drivers, IMU feedback, and closed-loop trajectory tracking to evaluate multi-axis dynamics in a compact form factor.

Gallery

What

The goal was to re-engineer the Ringbot's mechanical structure and create a fully functional protptype capable of navigating diverse terrains.

How

Mechanical Work: Designed and fabricated Ringbot's components utilizing SolidWorks and 3D Printing. Redesigned legs to accomadate four LiPo batteries and reprinted parts to improve fit and strenght. Assembly & Wiring: Integrated Dynamixel XM430 motors and soldered extensive wiring. Connected motors to a Raspberry Pi Zero 2W running Ubuntu 22.04 and ROS2 Humble. Software and Control: Robot was imported into MATLAB/Simulink and then simulated in Simscape Multibody. Trained RL agents to control standing and locomtion.

Results

Completed full assembly of robot with integrated leg and wheel modulues, successfully wired and connected all motors and sensors to Raspberry Pi, enabeling remote communications with delays as low as ~6-7ms
