Sandia Labs: Counterfeit Bolt Detection

Detecting counterfeit fasteners using imaging and machine learning.

Overview

Counterfeit bolts pose serious risks in aerospace, defense, and industrial supply chains. They can look identical to authentic parts but often fail due to poor materials, improper heat treatment, or missing/false markings. Current detection methods are either destructive, inconsistent, or too costly for scalable use. Our project aims to create a nondestructive, portable system that combines optical inspection with simple non-optical tests (weight, resistance, magnetism) to reliably detect counterfeit bolts. The system must remain lightweight, compact, and able to evaluate a bolt in minutes.

Proposed Design

Our design integrates optical imaging with a mechanical system to capture multiple bolt views while also measuring basic physical properties.

Imaging & Data

  • Controlled lightbox with diffuse lighting; fixed camera distance and angle.
  • Multi-view captures (top, side, head markings, thread profile).
  • Reference target for color and scale normalization.

Preprocessing

  • Background removal and pose alignment (fiducials/marker-based).
  • White balance, exposure normalization, and denoising.
  • ROI isolation: head markings, shank, threads.

Features & Model

  • Geometric: head flats, diameter, pitch, thread profile metrics.
  • Marking: OCR + template match for grade/manufacturer stamps.
  • Surface: texture/reflectance cues (LBP/GLCM) for material finish.
  • Baseline classifier (SVM/Random Forest) with confusion-matrix reporting.
  • Optional deep model (lightweight CNN or ViT) with data augmentation.

Evaluation

  • Train/val/test split with stratification by lot and manufacturer.
  • Metrics: precision/recall, F1, ROC–AUC; threshold tuned for low false negatives.
  • Explainability via saliency/feature importance for auditability.

Future Work

  • Collect larger, labeled datasets spanning multiple grades, finishes, and manufacturers.
  • Integrate 3D profiling (structured light or photogrammetry) for thread geometry verification.
  • Develop real-time inference on an embedded target (e.g., Jetson) with batch QA tooling.
  • Add anomaly detection to flag novel counterfeit patterns beyond trained classes.
  • Create operator UI: pass/fail dashboard, traceable reports, and lot-level analytics.
