Spaceship Rope Crawler

Overview

A compact mechanism inspired by spacecraft servicing systems, designed to traverse a tensioned line with secure gripping and controlled advance. Focus areas include drive geometry, load transfer, and control strategies for smooth motion and reliable holding.

Gallery

What

A line-traversing robot that grips and advances along a rope for inspection or transport tasks. Emphasis on grip reliability, weight, and modularity.

How

Designed cam/roller or toothed-drive interfaces, tested different grip materials, modeled load paths, and tuned motor control for smooth starts, stops, and slope transitions. Logged performance metrics for pull force and slip thresholds.

Results

Achieved consistent forward motion under varying rope tension, reduced slip events, and validated a control envelope for reliable traversal. Identified improvements for lighter chassis and faster serviceability.
