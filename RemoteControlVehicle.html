Remote Control Vehicle

Overview

A three-speed remote control vehicle featuring an automatic transmission (low, high, reverse) on a pickup-style chassis. The project explores mechanical design, linkage geometry, drivetrain efficiency, and closed-loop control for smooth gear changes under varying loads.

Gallery

What

This project involved creating a remote controlled vehicle equipped with a three gear transmission: Low, High, and Reverse. The vehicle features automatic gear shifting that is controlled through the remote, allowing smooth transitions between gears without manual intervention.

How

The vehicle was designed in Fusion 360 to model the chassis and custom transmission components. We implemented a three-speed automatic transmission using a dog-clutch and servo-driven linkage, enabling low, high, and reverse gears. Finite Element Enalysis (FEA) was used to verify stress in key drivetrain parts, and the design was iterated for strength and weight reduction.

Results

The remote control vehicle was fully assembled with the three-speed transmission operating as intended. The servo driven dog-clutch reliably shifted between low, high, and reverse gears under load, providing smooth transitions without excessive slip. The simulation results from FEA aligned with physical performance, validating the design choices. The project demonstrated that a compact automatic transmission can be successfully implemented at the RC scale, giving us valuable experience in CAD, drivetrain integration, and gear ratios.
